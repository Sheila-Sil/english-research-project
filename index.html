Apatosaurus
Research Summary

I’ve known about the Apatosaurus long before I had grasped topics like extinction, climate, or, honestly, much of anything. When I was younger, understanding it was simply my passion; its sheer scale felt comforting. In the backseat of my mom’s car, I would imagine highways stretching into the distance as the long, arching necks of the burly sauropods. It was like the modern world was reshaped into something ancient and alive.

"Concrete bending like vertebrae, cutting through the land. Infrastructure became anatomy."

The name Apatosaurus comes from Latinized Greek, meaning “deceptive lizard”. It has a background of human error. It was first described in 1877 by Othniel Charles Marsh during the infamous "Bone Wars."

The highways that once resembled its neck now fracture habitats rather than sustain them. They’re simply giants reduced to memory, study, and the quiet acknowledgement that even the largest beings are vulnerable to systems they cannot outgrow.
